1.2 Add the PVM Help modal (paste once near bottom of page)

Paste near the end of the template, before {% endblock %}:

<div class="modal fade" id="helpPvm" tabindex="-1" aria-hidden="true">
  <div class="modal-dialog modal-lg modal-dialog-scrollable">
    <div class="modal-content">
      <div class="modal-header">
        <h5 class="modal-title">PVM — Help (Price / Volume / Mix)</h5>
        <button type="button" class="btn-close" data-bs-dismiss="modal" aria-label="Close"></button>
      </div>

      <div class="modal-body">
        <p><b>What is PVM?</b><br>
        PVM explains <b>why</b> a customer’s revenue changed between a <b>Current</b> period and a <b>Baseline</b> period
        (Previous Period or Prior Year). It breaks the change into <b>Price</b>, <b>Volume</b>, and <b>Mix</b>.
        </p>

        <p><b>P = Price effect</b><br>
        Revenue change caused by <b>unit price changes</b> on items the customer already bought before.
        </p>

        <p><b>V = Volume effect</b><br>
        Revenue change caused by buying <b>more or fewer units</b> (quantity changes), assuming baseline prices.
        </p>

        <p><b>M = Mix effect</b><br>
        Revenue change caused by a different <b>basket</b>: new items bought now (not in baseline), lost items, or a shift to different items.
        </p>

        <hr>

        <p><b>How are the numbers calculated?</b><br>
        For each item we compute:<br>
        <span class="text-muted">P Curr = revenue / qty (current), P Base = revenue / qty (baseline)</span><br>
        Then:<br>
        <span class="text-muted">ΔRevenue = R1 − R0</span><br>
        <span class="text-muted">Volume effect = P0 × (Q1 − Q0)</span><br>
        <span class="text-muted">Price effect = Q0 × (P1 − P0)</span><br>
        <span class="text-muted">Mix effect = the remainder so that ΔRevenue = Price + Volume + Mix</span>
        </p>

        <p><b>How to interpret common cases</b></p>
        <ul>
          <li><b>Mix dominates:</b> Many items have Q Base = 0 (new items) or Q Curr = 0 (lost items). This means the basket changed.</li>
          <li><b>Price dominates:</b> Unit prices changed on existing items (discounts/pricelist changes).</li>
          <li><b>Volume dominates:</b> The customer bought more/less quantity of the same items.</li>
        </ul>

        <p class="text-muted mb-0">
          Tip: PVM requires Compare mode (Previous period or PY). If Compare is “None”, PVM cannot run.
        </p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>
