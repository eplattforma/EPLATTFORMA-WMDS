What is the problem with “MEDIAN6 including zeros”

Your current logic:

Take last 6 weeks → compute median → use as forecast
Example:
[0, 0, 1, 0, 2, 0]

Sorted:

[0, 0, 0, 0, 1, 2]

Median:

0

👉 Forecast = 0

❌ Why this breaks your system

Because in your business:

many SKUs sell occasionally
not every week
zeros are normal

So median is basically saying:

👉 “most weeks = zero → demand = zero”

Which is wrong.

✅ What you SHOULD understand
Median answers:

👉 “What does a typical week look like?”

But your reality is:

👉 “Demand happens over time, not every week”

So you need:

👉 Rate-based thinking
“How much demand happens over time?”
📊 Correct interpretation of same data
[0, 0, 1, 0, 2, 0]

Total = 3 units
Weeks = 6

3 / 6 = 0.5 per week

👉 This is the real demand

🧠 Key insight

👉 Zeros ≠ no demand
👉 Zeros = no sale that week

That’s a completely different thing.

⚠️ When median becomes dangerous

Median becomes wrong when:

many zeros exist
demand is intermittent
OOS may exist (even worse)
🔥 Simple rule (what to do instead)

You do not decide based on “number of zeros”

You decide based on:

👉 number of non-zero weeks
🚦 Practical rule (use this)

Look at last 6–8 weeks:

Case 1 — enough signal
non_zero_weeks ≥ 4

👉 OK to use normal methods
(but still rate-based is safer)

Case 2 — typical intermittent (your main case)
non_zero_weeks = 2–3

👉 Use:

forecast = total_units / total_weeks

👉 DO NOT use median

Case 3 — almost no signal
non_zero_weeks ≤ 1

👉 You do NOT have enough data

Use:

forecast = analogue_floor

(or max of rate + floor if needed)

💣 Why MEDIAN6 is especially bad for you

Because your system has:

low-frequency SKUs
uneven ordering patterns
potential OOS distortion

👉 median will:

kill forecasts to zero
stop ordering
create stockouts
reinforce wrong data
🧩 What you should replace in logic

Instead of:

median(last_6_weeks)

Use:

total_units / total_weeks
🧠 Even better (optional refinement)

If you want slightly smarter:

non_zero_avg = average of non-zero weeks
frequency = non_zero_weeks / total_weeks

forecast = non_zero_avg * frequency

👉 This equals the same as rate-based, but more explainable.

📌 Final understanding

👉 MEDIAN6 fails because it treats zeros as dominant signal

👉 But in your business:

zeros are noise
time-based demand is the signal
✅ One-line conclusion

👉 If there are many zero weeks, median will lie to you — use total demand over time instead.
