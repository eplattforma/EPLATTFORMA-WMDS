Help text (short, simple — paste into your in-app Help)

Why does Pricing Analytics revenue sometimes look lower than Customer 360?
Pricing Analytics (Price Index vs Market) benchmarks only the Top N items (default Top 50) for performance and accuracy. It also uses positive sales only by default (returns/credit notes are excluded unless you tick Include credits).
So the “Revenue” shown in Price Index is not your total customer sales — it is the revenue covered by the benchmarked Top N items.
Use the Coverage % to see what share of the customer’s gross positive sales is included in the benchmark.

1) Backend: enrich /pricing/api/price-index summary

File: blueprints/pricing_analytics.py
Function: api_price_index()

1.1 Add two extra “totals” queries (ALL items, not Top N)

Right after you build line_where (or right after top_n is read), add these:

# Always define a "positive sales" filter (for gross positive sales), independent of include_credits
line_where_pos = """
  sale_date BETWEEN :d_from AND :d_to
  AND customer_code_365 = :customer
  AND qty > 0 AND net_excl > 0
"""

# Net sales including credits/returns (for reconciliation vs 360)
line_where_all = """
  sale_date BETWEEN :d_from AND :d_to
  AND customer_code_365 = :customer
  AND qty <> 0
"""


Then execute the totals:

sql_totals = text(f"""
  SELECT
    COALESCE(SUM(net_excl), 0) AS gross_positive_sales_all_items,
    COALESCE(SUM(qty), 0) AS gross_positive_qty_all_items,
    COUNT(DISTINCT item_code_365) AS distinct_items_positive
  FROM dw_sales_lines_mv
  WHERE {line_where_pos}
""")

tot = db.session.execute(sql_totals, {
    "customer": customer, "d_from": d_from, "d_to": d_to
}).mappings().first()

gross_positive_sales_all = float(tot["gross_positive_sales_all_items"] or 0)
gross_positive_qty_all = float(tot["gross_positive_qty_all_items"] or 0)
distinct_items_positive = int(tot["distinct_items_positive"] or 0)

sql_net = text(f"""
  SELECT COALESCE(SUM(net_excl), 0) AS net_sales_all_lines
  FROM dw_sales_lines_mv
  WHERE {line_where_all}
""")

net_row = db.session.execute(sql_net, {
    "customer": customer, "d_from": d_from, "d_to": d_to
}).mappings().first()

net_sales_all_lines = float(net_row["net_sales_all_lines"] or 0)

1.2 Compute coverage of benchmarked basket

After your loop where you compute total_revenue (Top N only), add:

coverage_pct = (total_revenue / gross_positive_sales_all) if gross_positive_sales_all else None

1.3 Return the new fields in summary

Extend your existing summary = {...} with:

summary.update({
    "top_n": top_n,
    "benchmarked_sales_top_n": total_revenue,  # same as total_revenue, but clearer name
    "gross_positive_sales_all_items": gross_positive_sales_all,
    "net_sales_all_lines": net_sales_all_lines,
    "coverage_pct": coverage_pct,
    "distinct_items_positive": distinct_items_positive,
})


Keep your existing total_revenue if you want backward compatibility, but prefer displaying benchmarked_sales_top_n in UI.
