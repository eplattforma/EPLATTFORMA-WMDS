Below are visible, high-impact UI changes you can make to get much clearer separation between stops / invoice rows / discrepancy details (lines, colours, spacing). I’ll describe exactly what to change and what it achieves.

1) Make each Stop look like a “card” (strong separation)

Change

Wrap each stop in a card with:

1px border (subtle)

8–12px radius

12–16px outer margin between stops

Optional soft shadow (very subtle on dark theme)

Add a left accent bar (4–6px wide) on the stop header.

Colours

Card border: #2B3440

Card background: slightly different from page, e.g. #1C2430

Accent bar (based on stop health):

OK: #22C55E

Has discrepancy/returns: #F59E0B

Has outstanding: #EF4444

Why
Stops stop “blending” into one continuous dark block. The left colour bar gives instant scanning.

2) Split Header vs Body with a stronger divider

Change

Stop header background slightly lighter/darker than body.

Add a 2px divider line under the header (not 1px).

Colours

Header bg: #2A3340

Body bg: #1C2430

Divider: #3A4656 (2px)

Why
Users visually “lock” onto the header, then naturally read down.

3) KPI area (TOTAL/RETURNS/RECEIVED/METHOD/OUTSTANDING) as pills/tiles, not loose text

Change

Put each KPI into a small tile/pill with consistent width.

Keep label small and value big.

Give each KPI a tinted background, not just coloured text.

Colours (tints)

Received (green tint): bg rgba(34,197,94,0.12) text #22C55E

Returns (amber tint): bg rgba(245,158,11,0.12) text #F59E0B

Outstanding (red tint): bg rgba(239,68,68,0.12) text #EF4444

Total (neutral): bg rgba(148,163,184,0.10) text #E5E7EB

Method pill:

CASH: #22C55E tint

ONLINE: #60A5FA tint

Why
Right now the KPI values float and compete. Tiles make them “readable blocks”.

4) Fix the big empty white area (it breaks the dark UI)

In Stop 1 you have a large white blank panel.

Change (pick one)

Option A (best): If no discrepancy lines exist, collapse/hide the right panel entirely.

Option B: Show a dark placeholder box: “No discrepancy lines” + icon.

Option C: Keep the panel but match the stop body background (never pure white).

Why
That white rectangle is the biggest visual distraction on the screen.

5) Stronger table readability: zebra + row separators + numeric alignment

Change

Add zebra striping on invoice rows and item rows (very subtle).

Add a 1px row separator across full width.

Right-align all numeric columns and enable tabular numbers.

Colours

Zebra alt row: rgba(255,255,255,0.03)

Row divider: rgba(255,255,255,0.06)

Why
Makes scanning totals and discrepancies dramatically easier.

6) Highlight discrepancy cells with background, not only red text

Change

When discrepancy > 0, apply a red-tinted cell background.

Same for outstanding.

Example

Discrepancy cell bg: rgba(239,68,68,0.14)

Outstanding cell bg: rgba(239,68,68,0.18)

Normal cell bg: none

Why
Colour-only text is easy to miss; tinted blocks make issues pop instantly.

7) Make “Type” badges consistent and more meaningful

Right now you have badges like short, rejected.

Change

Use a consistent badge system:

SHORT (amber)

REJECTED (red)

OK (green/neutral)

Same size, same padding, same casing.

Why
Badges become a reliable visual language.

8) Improve the “Not Delivered” column clarity

“Not Del.” is short but still ambiguous.

Better

Label: ND (very compact) with tooltip “Not Delivered”

Or two columns:

ND Qty

ND € (amount)

Why
Users understand instantly whether it’s quantity or value.

9) Separate left invoice table and right item table more clearly

Change

Use a visible vertical divider and spacing:

Divider 2px + 16px padding on both sides.

Give the right panel its own mini header: “Discrepancy lines”.

Why
Currently the two zones feel glued together; this creates a clear “left summary / right details” layout.

10) Add a “Stop status” label near the stop title

Change

Next to the customer name show a single status pill:

OK

Partial

Has Outstanding

Has Returns

Why
One glance per stop tells you where to focus (especially useful when you have many stops).
