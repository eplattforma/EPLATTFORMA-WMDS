# Driver Receipt Rules — Keep It Simple

**The one rule to remember:**
> You can never cancel or lower a receipt the customer is already holding. Either get **all** the paper back and call the office, or write a manual receipt and bring everything back.

---

## When you collect payment

1. Enter the amount on the keypad.
2. The screen shows what the customer **owes** and what you **entered**.
   - **Green tick** = it matches. Tap **Confirm**. Done.
   - **Amber (short)** = customer is paying less than owed. Pick a reason (*paying part / dispute / no change*), then Confirm.
   - **Red (over)** = customer is paying more than owed. Check it's not a typo. If it's real, pick a reason and Confirm.
3. Print. Two slips come out: **CUSTOMER COPY** (hand to the customer) and **OFFICE COPY** (keep it — you hand it in at reconciliation).

**Once it's printed and given, that receipt is final.** The app will lock it.

---

## If a receipt is wrong

### The customer is still with you
- Take the **CUSTOMER COPY** back (if you reprinted, take every customer copy — the app knows how many exist). Keep your office copy.
- Call the office / admin to cancel it.
- Once cancelled, re-enter the correct amount, print, and hand over the new receipt.

### The customer has already left, or you have no signal / printer
- Do **not** try to lower or cancel the printed receipt — you can't.
- Write a **manual receipt** (from the numbered book) for the correct amount and give it to the customer.
- Keep the wrong printed slip(s).
- Bring the manual receipt and the wrong slips to reconciliation. The admin sorts it out when you return.

---

## At reconciliation

- Hand in your **office copies** for every receipt.
- Hand in every **customer copy** you took back for a cancellation — the count must match what the system printed.
- Hand in every manual receipt you wrote, with the matching amount.
- Your cash must match the receipts you issued.

---

## Why these rules exist

A printed receipt is proof for the customer. If it could be quietly changed or made to disappear, a customer holding a €500 slip could be told they paid €50 — or the reverse. Collecting the paper back and going through the office keeps you covered and keeps every euro traceable to you. Follow the rule and any dispute is settled in seconds by looking up the number.

**Never:** lower a receipt the customer holds • cancel without returning the paper • write a manual receipt you don't log.
