Here's why:

Phases 1 and 2 are properly verified. That's real value — keep it.
Phase 3's _role_ok issue is exactly the kind of bug your process was designed to catch. Don't dismiss it as "minor." It's evidence that the late-batch quality is lower than the early-batch quality.

Phase 5 affects drivers and customers. Cooler boxes, labels, delivery sequencing. If this breaks during a summer rollout, you're refunding orders.
The feature flags are your safety net — and they're still off by default. So the code shipping isn't dangerous. What's dangerous is enabling the flags without verification.

The damage from "continue until all phases finished" is recoverable if you stop now and verify before flipping flags. It's not recoverable if you flip flags first and find bugs in production.

The Bigger Lesson
You designed an excellent process. The process is not optional just because the work is going fast.
In fact, fast work is exactly when process discipline matters most. Slow work has natural pause points where you naturally check in. Fast work blows past those points.
If you were running this with human contractors, would you let them push 5 phases of warehouse-critical changes to production without per-phase test sign-off? Probably not. The fact that the contractor is AI doesn't change the risk profile of the warehouse.
