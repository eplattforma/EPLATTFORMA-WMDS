Overview

The Forecast & Replenishment system automatically predicts demand and calculates optimal reorder quantities for your inventory. It analyzes 26 weeks of sales history to classify demand patterns and recommend purchase orders.

Core Process:
  1. Analyze 26 weeks of historical sales
  2. Classify demand pattern (smooth, erratic, intermittent, lumpy, new/sparse)
  3. Select appropriate forecast method
  4. Calculate target stock level
  5. Fetch current inventory from PS365
  6. Recommend purchase quantity

Demand Classification

Items are automatically classified based on two metrics from 26-week history:

  • ADI (Average Demand Interval): Average number of days between sales
  • CV² (Coefficient of Variation Squared): Variability in demand quantities
Class Description ADI CV² Use Case
Smooth Regular, predictable demand < 1.32 < 0.49 High-volume staple items
Erratic Frequent sales, high variability < 1.32 ≥ 0.49 Popular items with variable quantity orders
Intermittent Infrequent, consistent quantities ≥ 1.32 < 0.49 Specialty items with occasional demand
Lumpy Infrequent, highly variable ≥ 1.32 ≥ 0.49 Project-based or custom orders
New/Sparse Fewer than 6 weeks of sales — — New products or items with minimal history
No Demand Zero sales in 26 weeks ∞ — Inactive/obsolete items

Forecast Methods

The system uses different calculation methods based on demand class:

MA8 (Moving Average of Last 8 Weeks)

Used for: Smooth, Erratic demand

Average of the 8 most recent weeks of demand Simple, stable forecast for predictable demand
MEDIAN6 (Median of Last 6 Weeks)

Used for: Intermittent, Lumpy demand

Middle value of the 6 most recent weeks Robust to outliers — better for variable demand
SEEDED (Seeded Analogue)

Used for: New/Sparse demand

When history is too short, finds similar items from: • Same supplier (preferred) • Same brand • Same item prefix Uses their average demand as a proxy
ZERO

Used for: No Demand

No forecast generated. Item has zero sales history.

Replenishment Calculation

Target Stock Formula
Target Stock = (Daily Forecast × Cover Days) + (Daily Forecast × Lead Time) + (Daily Forecast × Review Cycle) + Buffer Stock
Components Explained
  • Daily Forecast: Weekly forecast ÷ 7
  • Cover Days: Days of buffer inventory to hold (default: 7 days)
  • Lead Time: Days to receive order from supplier (from Supplier Mapping)
  • Review Cycle: Days between replenishment reviews (default: 1 day)
  • Buffer Stock: Safety stock for demand variability (default: 1 day of forecast)
Order Quantity Calculation
Raw Order = max(0, Target Stock − Net Available) where Net Available = On Hand + Incoming − Reserved

Then rounded to the nearest Order Multiple (case quantity) and enforced to meet Minimum Order Quantity (MOQ).

Typical Workflow

1
Set Supplier Parameters
Go to Admin Settings → Supplier Mapping and define:
  • Lead time in days (time to receive from supplier)
  • Review cycle (how often to review stock)
  • Order multiple (case quantity, if applicable)
  • Minimum order quantity (MOQ)
2
Run Forecast
Click "Run Forecast" on the Suppliers page. The system will:
  • Analyze 26 weeks of sales history for all active items
  • Classify demand patterns
  • Calculate forecasts
  • Fetch current stock levels from PS365
3
Review Results
Check the Suppliers page to see:
  • Recommended order quantities
  • Items flagged for manual review
  • Current stock vs. target stock
4
Investigate Review Flags
Items with flags (yellow badge) need attention. Common reasons:
  • Demand class change (erratic, new, lumpy)
  • Missing supplier mapping
  • Forecast changed significantly from last run
5
Create Purchase Orders
Use recommended quantities to create POs in your procurement system (or export from this interface).

Supplier Mapping

Each item needs a supplier to enable proper replenishment planning. The system uses this priority:

Supplier Resolution (in order):
  1. Primary: Supplier Mapping (if configured for the item)
  2. Fallback: DwItem.supplier_code_365 (if mapping missing)
  3. Missing: Item marked for review; replenishment skipped
Setting Up Supplier Mapping
  1. Go to Admin → Supplier Mapping
  2. Select item and assign supplier
  3. Enter lead time (days to receive from supplier)
  4. Enter review cycle (optional; default: 1 day)
  5. Enter order multiple (case qty, or 1 for individual units)
  6. Enter minimum order quantity (MOQ, if any)
  7. Save

Note: If using fallback (DwItem supplier), the system will log "fallback supplier used" in the review reason. Prefer explicit mapping for better control.

Review Flags & Reasons

Items are flagged for manual review when unusual conditions are detected. Review flags are rebuilt fresh on every forecast run.

Common Review Reasons
  • Demand class: Item is erratic, lumpy, or new/sparse demand
  • Trend (up/down): Demand increased or decreased by more than 10%
  • Forecast changed: Weekly forecast changed by more than 20% from previous run
  • Order rounding distortion: Rounding to order multiple caused >30% change
  • Missing supplier mapping: No supplier found for the item
  • Fallback supplier used: Using DwItem supplier instead of mapped supplier
  • No demand but stock exists: Item has zero sales but inventory on hand
  • Inactive item with recent sales: Item marked inactive but has recent demand

Important: Review flags are not carried over between runs. They are rebuilt from scratch based on current data only.

Running a Forecast

Manual Forecast Run
  1. Go to Forecast Workbench → Suppliers
  2. Click "Run Forecast" button (top right)
  3. System will process all active items (takes 1–5 minutes depending on volume)
  4. Status banner will show when complete
Automated Schedule

Forecasts also run automatically:

  • Daily at 5:00 AM: Full forecast run for all items
What to Check After a Run
  • Last run timestamp (verify it completed)
  • Total items processed
  • Items with review flags (yellow badges)
  • Unmapped items (items without a supplier)

Troubleshooting

Question: Why does an item have "SEEDED" forecast?

Answer: The item has fewer than 6 weeks of sales history. The system finds similar items (same supplier, brand, or prefix) and uses their demand as a proxy. Once the item has 6+ weeks of sales, it will be reclassified on the next run.

Question: Why is the recommended order quantity 0?

Answer: Current stock (on hand + incoming − reserved) already exceeds the target stock level. No reorder is needed at this time. Check back after the next forecast run or when stock levels drop.

Question: Why is an item flagged for review?

Answer: Check the review reason badge. Common causes are demand pattern changes, missing supplier info, or significant forecast shifts. Click the item to see details.

Question: How often should I update supplier lead times?

Answer: Update immediately if your supplier's lead time changes. Lead time directly affects target stock calculations, so accuracy is critical.

Question: Can I modify a single item's forecast?

Answer: Forecasts are recalculated on every run. To override for a specific item, you can manually adjust supplier mapping (lead time, MOQ, etc.) before the next run, or mark the item for special handling in your procurement process.

Question: What if my supplier mapping differs from DwItem supplier?

Answer: The system prioritizes your supplier mapping. If mapped, it uses that supplier's code for stock fetches and planning. DwItem supplier is only used as a fallback if no mapping exists. This is intentional — mapping gives you full control.

For additional support, contact your warehouse manager or system administrator.
