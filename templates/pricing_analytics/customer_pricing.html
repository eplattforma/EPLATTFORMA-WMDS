Price Index vs Market — Help

What this report does
Price Index compares the prices a customer paid for items in the selected period against a market reference price calculated from your own customer base.

For each item we show:
Customer Price = total net sales ÷ total quantity for that customer (excl VAT)
Market Price = the benchmark price for the same item (Median or Max)
Index = Customer Price ÷ Market Price
Δ €/unit and Δ € total = how far the customer is from the benchmark price

How “Market Median” is calculated
Market Median is calculated in two steps, per item:
1. Customer-average price per item: For each customer who bought the item in the period, we calculate: customer_item_price = SUM(net_excl) / SUM(qty)
2. Median across customers: We then take the median of those customer-average prices: market_median = median(customer_item_price across customers)
✅ This means each customer counts once, and the median is robust (a single extreme price does not distort the benchmark).

What is included / excluded
By default, Pricing Analytics uses positive sales only:
Included: lines where qty > 0 and net_excl > 0
Excluded: credit notes / returns (usually negative lines), zero-value lines, and zero-qty lines
If you tick Include credits, returns/credits are included for the customer totals (but market benchmarks still use positive sales only).

Why Price Index revenue may be lower than Customer 360
Price Index benchmarks only the Top N items (default Top 50) by customer revenue for performance and clarity. So the “Revenue” shown in Price Index is the revenue covered by those Top N items, not the customer’s full sales.

Use:
Gross Positive Sales (all items) = total positive sales for the customer in the period
Benchmarked Revenue (Top N) = the part included in Price Index
Coverage % = Benchmarked Revenue ÷ Gross Positive Sales

How to interpret the Index
Index = 1.00 → customer price is at the market reference
Index > 1.00 → customer price is higher than benchmark
Index < 1.00 → customer price is lower than benchmark

Benchmark selector (Median vs Max)
Median = “typical market” (recommended default for fair comparison)
Max = “ceiling observed in market” (useful for headroom checks; can be influenced by outliers)
